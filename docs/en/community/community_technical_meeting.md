# Technical Regular Meeting


Triton-Ascend holds regular community technical meetings as the core decision-making mechanism for project governance. Major matters such as technical direction, major proposal reviews, and version releases are discussed and decided in the technical meetings, with voting conducted by all `Maintainer`s. The meetings are open to all community members, and anyone can participate in the discussions, but decisions and voting are carried out only by `Maintainer`s.


## Meeting Content


- Technical direction discussion and decision-making
- Major proposal (RFC) review and voting
- Version release planning and timeline
- Community Issue and PR priority sorting
- Contributor nomination and removal discussion

## Voting Rules


- `Maintainer` who did not attend the meeting must submit their vote within 3 working days after the meeting; failure to vote within this period will be considered abstention.
- The condition for a vote to pass is that more than half of the participating `Maintainer` agree (abstentions are not counted in the total votes).
- The voting results are recorded in the meeting minutes.


## Decision Process


### Routine Decisions


Routine decisions in daily development (such as `Bug` fixes, small feature improvements) are reviewed and decided by the `Maintainer` of the relevant module in the `Issue` or `PR`, without the need for a meeting.


### Major Decisions


The following matters are considered major decisions and require collective discussion by `Maintainer` in the community technical meeting:


- Major architectural changes
- Breaking `API` changes
- Addition of core features or removal of existing features
- Major upgrades of third-party dependencies
- Version release planning


Major Decision-Making Process:


1. Submit an `RFC` (Request for Comments) describing the changes, scope of impact, and implementation plan
2. Discuss in the technical meeting
3. `Maintainer` makes a joint decision
