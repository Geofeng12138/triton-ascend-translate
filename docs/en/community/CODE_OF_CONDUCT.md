# Contributor Covenant


## Our Pledge


We pledge to keep our community friendly, safe, and fair for everyone.


We pledge to foster an environment that respects and promotes the dignity, rights, and contributions of every individual, regardless of race, ethnicity, caste, skin color, age, physical appearance, neurodiversity, disability status, sex or gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, language, philosophical or religious belief, nationality or social origin, socioeconomic status, educational background, or other identity attributes. All individuals who sincerely participate and adhere to this covenant are entitled to equal participation rights.



## Encouraged Behaviors


Although social norms may differ, we all strive to meet this community’s expectations of positive behavior. We also recognize that due to differences in culture, background, or native language, others may interpret our words and actions differently from what we intended.


With these considerations in mind, we commit to treating each other with care and respect, and to upholding the following shared values as our code of conduct:


1. Respect the **purpose** of our community, its activities, and ways of gathering
2. Interact with others with **goodwill and honesty**
3. Respect **different perspectives and experiences**
4. Take **responsibility** for one’s own words, actions, and contributions
5. Give and receive **constructive feedback** in a respectful manner
6. Commit to **making amends** when harm is caused
7. Engage in other actions that benefit the **well-being of the community**



## Restricted Behavior


We agree to restrict the following behaviors within the community. Engaging in these behaviors, threatening to engage in them, or promoting them are all considered violations of this Code of Conduct.


1. **Harassment**: Continuing to violate boundaries after they have been clearly expressed, or giving unwanted personal attention after being explicitly asked to stop.
2. **Personal Attacks**: Making insulting, demeaning, or contemptuous remarks directed at community members or groups.
3. **Stereotyping or Discrimination**: Judging others' character or behavior based on immutable identities or characteristics.
4. **Sexualization**: Engaging in intimate actions that are generally considered inappropriate within the community context or purpose.
5. **Breach of Confidentiality**: Sharing or exploiting others' personal or private information without permission.
6. **Harmful Behavior**: Committing, inciting, or threatening violence or other harm against any person or group.
7. Other behaviors that threaten the **well-being of the community**.



### Other Restricted Behaviors


1. **False Identity**: Impersonating others for any reason, or pretending to be someone else to evade regulatory measures.  
2. **Improper Attribution**: Failing to correctly indicate the source of contributed content.  
3. **Promotional Materials**: Sharing marketing or other commercial content in a manner inconsistent with community guidelines.  
4. **Improper Dissemination**: Failing to responsibly present content that includes, links to, or describes any other restricted behavior.



## Reporting Issues


Even when community members do their best to collaborate, conflicts may still arise. Not all conflicts involve violations of the Code of Conduct. This Code is intended to reinforce encouraged behaviors and norms, which help prevent conflicts and minimize harm.

Community administrators will take violation reports seriously and strive to respond in a timely manner. All reports of Code of Conduct violations will be investigated through methods including reviewing messages, logs, and recordings, or interviewing witnesses and other relevant participants. While prioritizing safety and confidentiality, community administrators will maintain as much transparency as possible in the investigation and enforcement process. To uphold these values, enforcement actions will be conducted in private settings involving the relevant parties; however, with the consent of all parties, notifying the entire community about the incident may also be part of the resolution.



## Handling and Remedying Harm


**[Note: The handling methods and remedial measures listed below are suggestions based on best practices in the enforcement of the Code of Conduct. If your community already has an established enforcement process, please ensure you modify this section to describe your own policy.]**


If the community moderators confirm through investigation that there has been a violation of this Code of Conduct, they will refer to the following "Progressive Action" mechanism to determine the most appropriate remedy based on the impact of the incident on the individuals involved and the community as a whole. Depending on the severity of the violation, lower-level measures may be skipped.


1) Warning  
   1) **Event**: A single or series of violations  
   2) **Consequence**: The community administrator will issue a written private warning  
   3) **Remedy**: Remediation may include a written private apology, acknowledging one's responsibility, or proactively confirming how to meet expectations in the future.  

2) Temporary Restriction  
   1) **Event**: Repeated violations that have already been warned about, or a first occurrence of a slightly more serious violation.  
   2) **Consequence**: A private written warning is issued, along with a time-limited cooling-off period to emphasize the severity of the situation and allow the involved community member time to process the event. The cooling-off period may restrict specific communication channels or interactions with certain community members.  
   3) **Remedy**: Remediation may include an apology, using the cooling-off period to reflect on one's behavior and its impact, and fully understanding how to re-enter the community space after the period ends.  

3) Temporary Suspension  
   1) **Event**: A pattern of repeated violations after multiple warnings from the community administrator, or a single serious violation.  
   2) **Consequence**: A private written warning is issued, along with conditions required for reinstatement. Typically, a temporary suspension aims to give the suspended individual time to reflect on their behavior and consider possible corrective actions.  
   3) **Remedy**: Conditions for remediation include respecting the intent of the suspension, meeting the specified conditions for reinstatement, and fully understanding how to reintegrate into the community after the suspension is lifted.  

4) Permanent Ban  
   1) **Event**: Repeated violations of the code of conduct where other graduated measures have failed to resolve the issue, or a violation severe enough that the community administrator deems the community unsafe with the member's continued presence.  
   2) **Consequence**: Revocation of access to all community spaces, tools, and communication channels. Generally, permanent bans should be used very rarely, with sufficient and compelling justification, and only as a last resort when other remediation measures have failed to change behavior.  
   3) **Remedy**: In such severe cases, no viable remediation pathway exists.


This tiered response measure is intended as a guideline and does not restrict community moderators from exercising their discretion and judgment in the best interest of the community.



## Scope of Application


This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces or other settings. Examples of representing the community include, but are not limited to: using an official email address; posting via an official social media account; or acting as a designated representative at an online or offline event.
