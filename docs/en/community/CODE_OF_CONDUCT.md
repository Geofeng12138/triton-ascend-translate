# Contributor Covenant


## Our Pledge


We pledge to keep our community friendly, safe, and fair for everyone.


We pledge to foster an environment that respects and promotes the dignity, rights, and contributions of every individual, regardless of race, ethnicity, caste, skin color, age, physical appearance, neurodiversity, disability status, sex or gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, language, philosophical or religious belief, nationality or social origin, socioeconomic status, educational background, or other identity attributes. All individuals who sincerely participate and adhere to this covenant are entitled to equal participation rights.



## Encouraged Behaviors


Although social norms may vary, we all strive to meet this community’s expectations for positive behavior. We also understand that due to differences in culture, background, or native language, others may interpret our words and actions differently from what we intended.


With these considerations in mind, we commit to treating each other with care and respect, and to upholding the following shared values as our code of conduct:


1. Respect the **purpose** of our community, its activities, and ways of gathering
2. Interact with others with **good faith and honesty**
3. Respect **different perspectives and experiences**
4. Take **responsibility** for your words, actions, and contributions
5. Give and receive **constructive feedback** in a respectful manner
6. Commit to **making amends** when harm is caused
7. Engage in other behaviors that benefit the **well-being of the community**



## Restricted Behaviors


We agree to restrict the following behaviors within the community. Engaging in these behaviors, threatening to engage in them, or promoting them is considered a violation of this Code of Conduct.


1. **Harassment**: Continuing to violate boundaries after they have been clearly expressed, or giving unnecessary personal attention after being explicitly asked to stop.
2. **Personal Attacks**: Making insulting, demeaning, or contemptuous remarks directed at community members or groups.
3. **Stereotyping or Discrimination**: Judging others' character or behavior based on immutable identities or characteristics.
4. **Sexualization**: Engaging in intimate actions that are generally considered inappropriate within the community context or purpose.
5. **Breach of Confidentiality**: Sharing or exploiting others' personal or private information without permission.
6. **Harmful Behavior**: Committing, inciting, or threatening violence or other harm against any person or group.
7. Other behaviors that threaten the **well-being of the community**.



### Other Restricted Behaviors


1. **False Identity**: Impersonating others for any reason, or pretending to be someone else to circumvent regulatory measures.
2. **Improper Attribution**: Failing to correctly indicate the source of contributed content.
3. **Promotional Materials**: Sharing marketing or other commercial content in a manner inconsistent with community guidelines.
4. **Improper Dissemination**: Failing to responsibly present content that contains, links to, or describes any other restricted behavior.



## Reporting Issues


Even when community members do their best to cooperate, conflicts may still arise. Not all conflicts involve violations of the Code of Conduct; this Code is intended to reinforce encouraged behaviors and norms, which help prevent conflicts and minimize harm.

All reports of Code of Conduct violations will be investigated through methods including reviewing messages, logs, and recordings, or interviewing witnesses and other relevant participants. While prioritizing safety and confidentiality, community administrators will maintain as much transparency as possible during the investigation and enforcement process. To uphold these values, enforcement actions will be conducted in private settings involving the relevant parties; however, with the consent of all parties, reporting the incident to the entire community may also be part of the resolution.



## Handling and Remedying Harm


**[Note: The handling methods and remedial measures listed below are suggestions based on best practices in the enforcement of the Code of Conduct. If your community already has an established enforcement process, please ensure you modify this section to describe your own policies.]**


If the community administrators confirm through investigation that there has been a violation of this Code of Conduct, they will refer to the following "graduated response" mechanism to determine the most appropriate remedy based on the impact of the incident on the individuals involved and the community as a whole. Depending on the severity of the violation, lower-level response measures may be skipped.


1) Warning  
   1) **Event**: A single or series of violations  
   2) **Consequence**: The community administrator will issue a written private warning  
   3) **Remedy**: Remediation methods include a written private apology, acknowledging one's own responsibility, or proactively confirming how to meet expectations in the future.  

2) Temporary Restriction of Activity  
   1) **Event**: Repeated violations that have already been warned about, or a first occurrence of a slightly more serious violation.  
   2) **Consequence**: A private written warning is issued, along with a time-limited cooling-off period to emphasize the severity of the situation and allow the relevant community member time to process the event. The cooling-off period may restrict access to specific communication channels or interactions with certain community members.  
   3) **Remedy**: Remediation may include an apology, using the cooling-off period to reflect on one's behavior and its impact, and fully understanding how to re-enter the community space after the period ends.  

3) Temporary Suspension  
   1) **Event**: A pattern of repeated violations after multiple warnings from the community administrator, or a single serious violation.  
   2) **Consequence**: A private written warning is issued, along with conditions required for reinstatement. Typically, a temporary suspension aims to give the suspended individual time to reflect on their behavior and consider possible corrective actions.  
   3) **Remedy**: Conditions for remediation include respecting the intent of the suspension, meeting the specified conditions for reinstatement, and fully understanding how to reintegrate into the community after the suspension is lifted.  

4) Permanent Ban  
   1) **Event**: Repeated violations of the code of conduct where other graduated measures have failed to resolve the issue, or a violation so severe that the community administrator determines the community cannot be safe with the member remaining.  
   2) **Consequence**: Revocation of access to all community spaces, tools, and communication channels. Generally, permanent bans should be used very rarely, must have sufficient and compelling reasons, and should only be implemented as a last resort when other remediation methods have failed to change behavior.  
   3) **Remedy**: In such severe cases, no viable remediation path exists.


This tiered handling approach is intended as a guideline and does not restrict community moderators from exercising their discretion and judgment in the best interests of the community.



## Scope of Application


This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public or other settings. Examples of representing the community include, but are not limited to: using an official email address; posting via an official social media account; or acting as a designated representative at an online or offline event.
